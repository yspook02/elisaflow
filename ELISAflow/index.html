How to Use ELISAflow

STEP 1Plate Converter

  • Upload your raw plate reader CSV — single or multi-plate, any wavelength
  • Configure plate metadata: date, antigen, immunogen per plate
  • Assign wells visually on the interactive 96-well grid: blanks, PBS, positive/negative controls, sample groups, dilution series
  • Set wavelength, reference subtraction, and blank background correction in Settings
  • Preview the processed data table, then export a structured CSV

STEP 2ELISA Analyser

  • Upload the CSV from the Converter — or any CSV in the format: Experiment, Dilution, Negative Control, Mouse1, Mouse2...
  • Each experiment generates one OD vs Dilution chart with error bars and a Negative Control-based cutoff line
  • Enable statistical tests (t-test, ANOVA), EC50 curve fitting, and outlier detection in the options panel
  • Adjust chart size, colour palette, and axis scale per chart
  • Print or export PDF — each chart gets its own page

💡 The two tools are designed as a pipeline: Converter produces the exact CSV format that Analyser expects. Use the sample CSV download in the Analyser if you want to test without running the Converter first.
